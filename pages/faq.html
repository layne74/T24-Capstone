FROM THE BEGINNING

My name is Layne Hutchings, although I would have guessed you already know that, and I am a racing driver for fun. My journey all began at a young age when my parents did the whole "if you do well in school and do your chores, we will buy you something nice". So one day my folks sat my brother and I down, praised us for our marks and all that jazz and presented us with a glorious Playstation 1 and Gran Turismo 2. I was hooked after the first race. I finished the game including all of the endurence races. Later on we got a PS2 with Gran Turismo 4 and a steering wheel and pedal set. I spent far to many hours on that game. Once again finishing it, aswell as the 24 hour endurance races.

Once I had gotten a little older I was promoted so to speak. My father would let me sit on his lap and let me steer the family Kombi, in an enclosed area of course. I would steer and he would manage the pedals, not that I would be able to reach them anyway. This taught me corner judjement and general timing in a real vehicle. I loved it. It got to the point where he would fetch us from school, we would stop at a corner shop and I would steer the car home, granted it was just down the road but hey, I was "driving" a real car on the road. My father being the owner of a mechanic shop, I would watch as he stripped motors, rebuilt cars and seemingly fix everything, hence the interest in cars.

One of his best friends started to race at Killarney, so my father helped him with his car such as fixing it and maintaining it. After a good few years he decided to build his own racing car and join his buddy. We would go on to watch them race for about 6 years. During that period I had bought myself a proper steering wheel and pedal set for my pc and was taking part in small but highly competitive online racing events and doing quite well. Around that time I was 21 years old and itching to join in on the action, the real action. The idea stuck around for about a year when somebody was selling their raceing car, a 2l 8v Volkswagen Golf Mk2. He claimed the motor was just rebuilt but he couldnt get the thing to start and I bought it for R 23k, which is a steal as it was fully kitted and ready for the track. Well it took my father roughly 2 hours to get it started.

THE SHORT AND SWEET

I played an absurd amount of racing games and was exposed to cars and engines throughout my childhood. I eventually bought a race car. I then crashed that car due to steering failure and built another one. I have been racing for about 4 years and am constantly improving, both my skills and the car.

I have won 1 championship in my class, and was in close contension for the other three. I plan on winning the next one. We have built up a Golf mk1 for rallycross that my father and I are sharing with each other, as we arn't it it for the points, just for fun.
